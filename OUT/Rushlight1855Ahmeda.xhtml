Song of the Mosquito I am a young mosquito, just come to light, To try my skill in humming, and show my power to bite, And no one dares come near me, while I'm winging thru' the air, For all dread the young mosquito, the ugly, old and fair. But I fly so quickly towards them, hid by the failing light, That they know not, I am with them till I greet them with a bite. I fly on a summers evening to a weary sick room, And to his aching cranium, come buzzing through the gloom. I light upon his pallid brow and quick begin to bite, And fan him with my gauzy wings, throughout the livelong night. He lifts his hand, and strikes his brow, with a stealthy muttered curse, And then I sing, "Good sir, don't fret, 'twill make your fever worse. The doctor walks in slowly, with a long, and heavy bill, For bleeding his poor patient and making him more ill. The sick man greets the doctor with a faint and feeble smile, And tells him "the mosquitoes have plagued him a long while He knows not, if he wished me, I would bleed him everyday. Like the Doctor, I'd present my bill but never ask for pay. I lit one summer evening ofon a lovely maidens hand, At her feet there knelt a lover the noblest in the land. He began to press his suit but I began to bite, The maiden started quickly, but I would not take my flight. She raised her hand and shook it, and cried, "Do go away," The lover thought 'twas meant for him, and rose and went for aye. I greet the ears of many with my sweet melodious hum, And when they are most happy, to plague them then I come, I buzz, and hum, and bite them, till I am driven away. But they find that I come back again and longer still I stay, And they hasten from my presence while I frolic in the air, For all dread the young mosquito, the ugly, youngold and fair.
Ship News Port of Norton July 11th

Arrived

Ship Wheaton. Maltby. Land of Knowledge, laden with Good Sense (cents) from the mint of Sound Understanding.

Brig. Euclid, Cragin. Sunny Side, laden with blackboards, explanations, and crocodile tears.

Brig Physiology, Lambert. Catacombs of Rome, laden with skeletons.

Cleared

Ship Bulter, Metcalf, laden with graduates for the "Wide, Wide, World."

Barque Comstock. Christopher Colombus, intends making further discoveries, carries out a quantity of yarn to spin with the natives.

Barque Calisthenics, Cutler, for the West laden with sashes, wreaths etc.

Disasters

No date. Ship Holmes, Sawyer, while passing Cape lookout, was struck by a gale, carried away jib-stove bulwarks, lost main-top gallant mast and two men. The poor sailors hearts utterly failed them. We understand the H. has been taken to Lake Superior for repairs.

Notice to Mariners

Light-boats. No. 1, No.2, No.9, No.13, and No.29, Hardingdate, Elmesside, Akinside, Westville, will be removed for the approaching 7 weeks.

Advertisements

For Sale. A prime lot of extra size, remarkably clear ideas. Sold for no-fault, the present owner being about to leave Wheaton Sem. has no further use for them.

Lost. On Saturday morning, by a young lady, while writing her composition; a link from a chain of thought.

Lost. On Thursday last, while ascending the steep and rugged hill of science - a persevering spirit; Also a small share of patience. Whoever finds the above articles will please them at 32 Trial Lane.

Lost. Somewhere between the hours of 9 and 12 yesterday, an opportunity for doing good. whoever will return the same uninjured shall receive the thanks of the unfortunate loser.

Wanted immediately! A good stock of Patience, Perserance, and Determination; for all which the highest premium will be paid.

Political Notices

The "Know Nothings" belonging to this institution are requested to meet in the Hall over Gymnasium, on the 32nd of the present month.

Several important subjects are to be considered one of which is the duty of refusing to use all text books made by foreigners or descendants of foreigners. N.B. None of the trustees are expected to be present.
